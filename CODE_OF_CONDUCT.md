# Code of Conduct 🤝

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open, welcoming, and toxic-free environment, we pledge to make participation in the `ez-cpp` project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of their background or experience level in C++.

## The Code Standard

`ez-cpp` was built to eliminate suffering and make C++ fun again. Therefore, we expect our community to reflect that same energy:

1. **No Elitism:** We do not tolerate gatekeeping. Whether someone has been coding in C++ for 10 years or 10 minutes, their questions and contributions are valid. Comments like *"Just use standard C++ bro"* or *"Skill issue"* will not be tolerated.
2. **Be Constructive, Not Destructive:** If you find a flaw in the library, point it out respectfully and suggest a fix. Don't just bash the code.
3. **Keep it Professional & Kind:** No insults, public shaming, or derogatory language. We are all here to build cool things and make life easier.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior. 

If someone is violating this code of conduct, the maintainers reserve the right to remove comments, close issues, or permanently ban the user from contributing.

Let's keep the vibe check positive! 🚀
